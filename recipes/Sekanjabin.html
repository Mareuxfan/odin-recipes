Ingredients

Steps

  1. Pour the sugar and water into a saucepan. Place over high heat stir to dissolve the sugar and bring to a simmer. Boiling the syrup over medium-low heat prevents the sugar from crystallizing.
  2. Pour the sugar and water into a saucepan. Place over high heat stir to dissolve the sugar and bring to a simmer. Boiling the syrup over medium-low heat prevents the sugar from crystallizing.
  3. Add mint sprigs to the warm and simmering syrup.
  4. Continue heating until the syrup thickens. To make sure our syrup has the right thickness to add vinegar, just take a spoonful of the syrup and pour a drop of it into a glass of cold water. If it settles, it means that it has a suitable thickness, but if it dissolves in water, it means that it still needs to be simmered for a while.
  5. When the syrup reaches the right thickness, add the vinegar and let it simmer for another 10-15 minutes. Then take the mint leaves out of the syrup and pass the syrup through a strainer.
  6. Pour the syrup into a clean glass container without moisture and put it in the refrigerator.
